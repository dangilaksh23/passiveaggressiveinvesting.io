02The Financial Picture

My current financial snapshot for Clean Science shows some gaps, with revenue and order book for the current fiscal year (FY2024-25) not yet reported. The prior year's revenue stood at ₹791.485 Cr. This lack of recent data makes it challenging to assess current financial momentum, although the management tone remains bullish.

The company is actively investing in its future, with a Capex of ₹141.95 Cr. Management expects to introduce 2-3 new performance-focused molecules and commission a new chemical plant in 2025-26, anticipating a meaningful contribution to consolidated financial performance, especially from HALS and pharma intermediates. This suggests a forward-looking strategy that requires upfront investment.

From a valuation perspective, the PE ratio stands at 37.2, which is somewhat high given the current lack of reported FY25 revenue, but its ROE of 15.3% and ROCE of 20.6% indicate decent operational efficiency. My framework notes clean governance and a Big4 auditor (Price Waterhouse Chartered Accountants LLP), which are positives.

03Why It's Stage 4 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data.

Clean Science is currently in Stage 4, characterized by a declining price trend, as evidenced by its 1-year return of -46.21%. This technical weakness likely stems from a combination of factors, including broader market sentiment towards the chemicals sector, and potentially, the market's reaction to the lack of reported revenue and order book for the current fiscal year. While management is bullish on future prospects, the market appears to be in a wait-and-see mode, re-rating the stock downwards until tangible signs of growth materialize from the ongoing Capex and new product introductions. The high PE ratio might also be undergoing a correction in the absence of fresh positive triggers.

What Would Move This to My Active List

  • Clear and positive revenue and order book updates for FY2024-25, demonstrating renewed financial momentum.
  • Successful and timely commissioning of the new chemical plant and introduction of new molecules, with early signs of meaningful contribution to financials.
  • A confirmed shift in its technical stage, moving out of Stage 4 and establishing a new base or uptrend.
  • Further clarity and execution on the "strong demand in HALS and pharma intermediates" translating into concrete sales growth.
