Thesis & Key Metrics

Core Thesis
Gravita owns the most defensible position in India's EPR recycling landscape: regulatory mandates across 4–5 waste streams create captive demand floors, the RMIL acquisition adds the fifth stream (copper) at 0.62x revenue, and the company earns a structural premium over commodity recyclers because it processes regulated waste that producers are legally compelled to recycle. The thesis is structural, not cyclical — EPR compliance doesn't pause when the economy slows.

Valuation & RMIL Acquisition

Gravita's multi-stream EPR platform justifies a premium over single-material recyclers. The correct peer group is not commodity processors — it is EPR-linked, mandate-protected waste businesses. At 26–31x P/E, the market is pricing a modest premium for EPR protection; the bull case is that Non-Ferrous EPR (April 2026 activation) creates a structural demand floor that peers cannot access.

RMIL Acquisition Rationale
Rashtriya Metal Industries — copper alloys, 31,200 MTPA capacity, 40% export. Acquired for ₹565 Cr against ₹910 Cr revenue (0.62x revenue multiple). Adds the copper stream ahead of Non-Ferrous EPR activation. Deal expected to close by March 31 2026. At 0.62x revenue, this is an opportunistic price for a business with established export relationships.
Non-Ferrous EPR — April 2026 Catalyst
Non-Ferrous EPR regulations mandating copper, aluminium, and alloy recycling targets activate April 2026 — simultaneous with RMIL close. Gravita is the only organised recycler with multi-metal capability already in place. Producer companies must either build recycling infrastructure (capital-intensive) or contract with a qualified recycler. Gravita's network makes the contract route the default outcome.

The re-rating risk is clear: at ₹1,357 the entry was compelling; at ₹1,636 the margin of safety has compressed. A formal 12M price target requires running the full valuation model against RMIL-inclusive FY27 revenue and confirming Stage 2. No target is published here pending that re-score.

Risk Register

  • Hard Risk RMIL Integration Failure: At ₹565 Cr acquisition cost, a failed integration could impair ₹100–150 Cr annually in expected copper-stream contribution. RMIL has 40% export exposure — if global copper prices compress, the acquisition adds revenue volatility rather than EPR-protected margin.
  • Hard Risk Non-Ferrous EPR Delay or Dilution: Regulations activating April 2026 could face implementation lag or enforcement gaps (as seen in plastic EPR). If producers are not penalised for non-compliance, the mandated demand floor does not materialise and C1 scoring rationale weakens.
  • Hard Risk Price Run-Up Compression: Stock has moved +20% since scoring CMP. At ₹1,636, the P/E of ~30–31x is no longer "cheap" relative to peers. Any earnings disappointment in a RMIL integration quarter could re-rate the stock down 15–20%. Entry discipline required.
  • Watch Commodity Lead Price Cycle: Lead recycling (~50% of revenue pre-RMIL) is exposed to LME lead prices. A global commodities downcycle could suppress both revenue and margins even if EPR volumes hold.
  • Watch Collection Network Scalability: India's informal waste-picker ecosystem supplies a large share of input material. Formalising this into a reliable supply chain at scale — especially for copper — requires operational discipline that RMIL as a standalone never needed to demonstrate.
  • Watch Promoter Governance: Promoter stake ~65% is adequate, but Gravita has limited institutional analyst coverage relative to its market cap. Information asymmetry risk is higher than for heavily covered mid-caps.
