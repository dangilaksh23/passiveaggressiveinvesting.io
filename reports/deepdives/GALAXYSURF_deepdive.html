03Why It's Stage 4 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data.

Galaxy Surfactants is firmly in Stage 4, characterized by a declining price and a 1-year return of -23.26%. My technical analysis framework, with a TT Score of 0/8 and an 'avoid' signal, confirms this downtrend. This could be attributed to a combination of factors: broader sector rotation out of chemicals, specific headwinds in the Home & Personal Care raw material space affecting demand or margins, or a period of slower growth as reflected by the missing current revenue data. The market appears to be discounting future prospects or reacting to recent performance, pushing the stock into a clear downtrend. I need to be honest with myself that the technical picture is poor, regardless of the underlying business quality.

What Would Move This to My Active List

  • Consistent and transparent reporting of positive revenue growth and a healthy order book, indicating a reversal in financial momentum.
  • A clear shift in the technical stage from Stage 4 to Stage 2, supported by strong institutional accumulation and price action.
  • Tangible evidence of success from their strategic initiatives in the Americas and Europe, leading to improved margins and profitability.
  • A significant improvement in ROE and ROCE, demonstrating enhanced capital efficiency and profitability.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • Any signs of governance issues or adverse findings from SEBI investigations.
  • A structural decline in demand for surfactants and specialty care ingredients, indicating a permanent shift in the industry.
  • Significant promoter selling or pledging of shares, eroding my conviction in their long-term commitment.
  • Continued stagnation or deterioration in financial performance without a clear path to recovery, despite management's bullish guidance.
04Where I Stand Today
Personal Stance
Today, I am not holding any position in Galaxy Surfactants. My current stance is strictly observational. While the underlying business quality, global footprint, clean governance, and strong investor backing are compelling, the technical Stage 4 status and the lack of recent revenue/order book data mean this is firmly on my "watching" list, not my "active" list. I am not looking to engage with this stock while it is in a clear downtrend.
My interest is purely in identifying fundamentally strong companies that are temporarily out of favor, anticipating a future technical turnaround. For Galaxy Surfactants, I need to see concrete evidence of financial improvements and a significant shift in price action before considering any engagement. This note serves as a reminder to track its progress and look for those specific catalysts that would indicate a true reversal of fortunes, both fundamentally and technically.
