The HVDC Milestone
First Indian company to win a PGCIL HVDC transformer repair order. HVDC (High Voltage Direct Current) transformer repair was previously the exclusive domain of ABB, Siemens, and Alstom globally. TARIL qualifying for this work is a gateway event — once you are on the PGCIL approved vendor list for HVDC, the follow-on orders compound. This is not captured in current earnings and represents a structural long-term upgrade to TARIL's competitive moat.
Why the Q4 Sell-Off Is Misread
Q4 FY26 PAT ₹89 Cr (-5% YoY) triggered an ~8% sell-off. Retail reading a single quarter as evidence of deterioration. Full-year FY26 PAT is ₹225 Cr (+20% YoY) — the quarter is noise within an accelerating annual trend. FII selling is macro-driven (broad FII outflow from India), not TARIL-specific. DII at 0.04% means there is no domestic institutional base to absorb FII selling — this creates the retail-dominated downdraft. The business has not changed.
02 Valuation — Cheap at 35x with 23% Revenue Growth

TARIL trades at approximately 35x P/E on FY26 earnings (₹225 Cr PAT on ~₹2,400 Cr revenue). For a capital goods company with 23% revenue growth, 2x order book coverage, and a newly qualified HVDC product line, 35x is not expensive — it is cheap. Comparable transformer companies in India trade at 40–55x on lower growth rates.

The complication is the absence of a formally published 6M price target in this report. The order book and inquiry pipeline (₹23,000 Cr) suggest FY28E PAT of ₹350–400 Cr is achievable if conversion rates hold. At 35x forward P/E on ₹375 Cr, that implies a fair value of ~₹750–800 Cr PAT × 35x = market cap of ₹13,000–14,000 Cr. TARIL's current market cap at ₹305 CMP implies meaningful upside — but this requires a formal deep dive to set a defensible target price.

04 Risks & What Would Invalidate the Thesis

Hard Invalidators (Exit Triggers)

  • Stop ₹270 hit Close below ₹270 — exit the position. No exceptions. Stage 4 stocks can trend lower for extended periods; the stop protects against a value trap scenario.
  • QIP funds diverted from stated capex ₹205 Cr of the ₹500 Cr QIP raise is still undeployed. If deployment is delayed beyond Q2 FY27 or directed to purposes other than the announced capacity expansion, governance flag is triggered.
  • RDSS scheme budget cut or delayed disbursement >2 quarters TARIL's order book depends on DISCOMs having RDSS funds. A central government cut or state-level disbursement freeze directly reduces new order flow.

Soft Watch Points (Re-evaluate, Don't Exit)

  • DII holding stays at 0.04% Without domestic institutional support, any FII selling creates outsized price impact. Watch quarterly shareholding patterns — DII rising above 2% would indicate institutional recognition and provide a price floor.
  • HVDC order follow-on timeline The PGCIL HVDC milestone is a qualification, not a revenue event yet. Monitor whether follow-on HVDC orders are announced within 12 months. If not, the strategic value of the milestone remains theoretical.
