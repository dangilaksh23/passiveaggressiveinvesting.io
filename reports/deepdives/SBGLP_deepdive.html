Suratwwala Business Group is a Pune-based developer run by founder Jatin Suratwala (30+ years in real estate). Two revenue streams: residential and commercial project development (the main business) and a solar subsidiary doing EPC and PPA contracts. Only 40 employees for ₹143Cr annual revenue — a leaner operation than almost anything else at this size in real estate.

The company came onto my radar through a sector-wide scan of real estate names on 28 Jun 2026. The KYB layer was already extracted from the FY2025 annual report, so I have reasonably good visibility on the business model and governance. This is not a name I have tracked for long — context is FY26 numbers + annual report analysis only.

Valuation as I See It

The valuation picture looks cheap on most cuts. Here is the five-year P&L I am looking at:

FY25 looks like a collapse but it was a project delivery gap. Real estate revenue under Ind AS 115 is recognised on handover, not on cash collection. The company was collecting advances from customers throughout FY25 (visible as ₹44Cr in "other liabilities" on the FY25 balance sheet) — it just had fewer completions that year. FY26 was the delivery year: ₹143Cr revenue, best ever, with Q4 FY26 alone at ₹59Cr.

The quarterly run-rate since Jun 2025 has been ₹16Cr → ₹26Cr → ₹42Cr → ₹59Cr — a staircase. At CMP ₹25.9 and FY26 EPS ₹2.19, the PE is 11.9×. If FY27 sustains even the Q3 FY26 run-rate (₹42Cr/quarter), the PE compresses further from here.

The CFO caveat. Operating cash flow has been negative for five consecutive years. I think about this carefully every time I look at this name. In real estate, negative CFO does not automatically mean the business is burning cash — customer advances flow in as liabilities, project inventory flows out as investment. But it does create a dependency on continued new bookings and a sensitivity to any credit tightening. The D/E of 0.8× and interest cover of 51× give adequate headroom today. My watch point is D/E — if it climbs above 1.5×, the narrative changes.

What I cannot tell from the data. There are no screener peers populated for SBGLP — it is too small and too regional for comparative data to be auto-populated. I am making a valuation judgement against the broader RE sector median, not a peer group. That introduces uncertainty I want to acknowledge.

The stock peaked somewhere in the ₹45–49 range — likely when FY24 results (₹72Cr revenue, ₹28Cr PAT, ROCE 55%) were the most recent data point. It then fell as FY25 revenue collapsed to ₹36Cr, and the price has not recovered despite FY26 revenue exceeding FY24 by 2×.

That divergence — fundamentals getting better, price not following — is what put this on my radar. But I have been wrong enough times holding fundamentally improving companies through extended Stage 4 periods that I now require the chart to show me something before I act. A stock at –48% from high can go to –60% or –70% before the turn. I do not have the conviction to hold through that.

What would make the chart more interesting: the stock recovering above ₹30–32 on meaningful volume with rising OBV, and then consolidating there rather than fading. That would suggest the FY26 delivery story is being absorbed by the market. Until then, I am watching, not acting.

What Could Change My Mind

Things that would strengthen my view:

Positive signal
Q1 FY27 revenue ≥₹35Cr sustaining the trajectory. The FY26 run was partially catch-up from a trough — I need to see it hold, not just spike.
Positive signal
CFO turning positive for the first time. Would suggest the project pipeline is delivering cash, not just accounting revenue.
Positive signal
A bulk deal or MF entry — any smart-money signal would validate the thesis independently of my own read.
Positive signal
Chart showing Stage 1 base: stock above ₹30–32 on volume, consolidating, not fading. Price and fundamentals re-aligning.

Things that would weaken or reverse my view:

Hard re-think
D/E crossing 1.5×. The current borrowing story is a manageable FY25 capex cycle. A second debt surge would change the risk profile entirely.
Hard re-think
Any auditor qualification. A mid-tier auditor for a ₹450Cr company is already a minor concern — a KAM turning into a qualification would be serious.
Watch point
Promoter selling even a small stake. Zero pledge + 73% holding is the governance anchor. Any reduction before institutional money validates the thesis would concern me.
Watch point
Pune real estate demand softening — the company is entirely Pune-region. A city-specific slowdown (interest rate shock, inventory glut) hits SBGLP harder than diversified national developers.
Watch point
CFO remaining deeply negative into FY27 with rising borrowings — the current negative CFO story works only if project deliveries continue. If they slip again like FY25, the cash equation gets tighter.
Watch point
New CFO and Company Secretary quality — both left in FY25. The incoming replacements I have not seen data on. Management quality at this size is the founder, but a weak finance function is a real operational risk.
05
Where I Stand Today

I do not currently hold this position.

Where I stand: the business quality is genuinely interesting — a 31% ROCE real estate developer at 11.9× PE with accelerating quarterly revenues is not something I see every day. But I have learned to separate "this business is good" from "now is the right moment to own it." The chart is in Stage 4. The institutional layer is empty. Those two things together mean the price could stay depressed or go lower regardless of how good the underlying economics are.

I am tracking this actively. What would move me from watching to acting is a combination of: Q1 FY27 revenue sustaining the FY26 trajectory, and the chart showing a constructive base at current levels or slightly higher. I am not in a hurry. The FY26 results are fresh and the market has not priced them in yet — if the thesis is real, there will be time to act after the chart gives me something to work with.

The maximum I would consider for a position of this size and liquidity profile, if all the above conditions were met, would be a small starter allocation — this is not a name I would go large on at any point given the illiquidity and the single-city concentration.
