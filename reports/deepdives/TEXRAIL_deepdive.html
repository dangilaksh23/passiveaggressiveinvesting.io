02The Financial Picture

My current data shows a prior fiscal year revenue of ₹3502.87 Cr, but the current fiscal year (FY25) revenue and order book figures are not yet available or reported as 'None Cr'. This significant gap in current financial data makes a precise assessment of recent performance challenging. However, the company has undertaken substantial Capex of ₹532.53 Cr, suggesting significant investment in future growth and capacity expansion.

Looking at the profitability metrics, the ROE stands at 7.63% and ROCE at 11.4%. While these are not exceptionally high, they indicate a degree of operational efficiency. The PE ratio of 21.7 seems reasonable given the sector's potential. Crucially, I note a Debt-to-Equity ratio of 'None', which is a very strong positive, indicating a debt-free or very low-debt balance sheet, providing significant financial flexibility.

Management's tone is bullish, targeting significant freight volumes by FY26 and 2030, alongside ambitious export growth in Africa, Southeast Asia, and Europe within 2-3 years. They've even provided specific project completion dates, which is good for accountability. This forward-looking guidance, however, needs to be supported by visible execution and improved financials in the upcoming quarters.

03Why It's Stage 4 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data.

Texmaco Rail is currently in a clear Stage 4 downtrend, as evidenced by its -34.62% return over the past year. This decline could be attributed to several factors. The lack of current FY25 revenue and order book data might be causing investor uncertainty, as the market dislikes a vacuum of information. Broader sector rotation away from infrastructure stocks, or profit-booking after a previous run-up, could also be contributing. While management is bullish, the market seems to be waiting for concrete execution and improved profitability metrics to justify a re-rating.

What Would Move This to My Active List

  • Clear and significant improvement in reported revenue and order book for FY25 and beyond, showing strong growth.
  • Demonstrated progress on global expansion initiatives, with tangible new orders from international markets.
  • Consistent improvement in profitability metrics (ROE, ROCE, margins) indicating better operational efficiency.
  • A shift in its technical posture to Stage 1 or 2, indicating accumulation and the start of a new uptrend.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A sustained decline in its order book or revenue without clear reasons or recovery plans.
  • Any deterioration in governance standards, such as promoter pledging or adverse SEBI findings.
  • A structural shift in demand for rail infrastructure, or increased competitive pressure severely impacting its market share.
  • Persistent low profitability and inability to convert its order book into higher revenue or better margins.
04Where I Stand Today
Personal Stance
As of today, I am not holding any position in Texmaco Rail & Engineering Limited. This company remains firmly on my watchlist, specifically due to its current Stage 4 technical status, indicating a declining price trend. While the long-term potential in India's infrastructure and rail sector is undeniable, and the company has strong institutional backing, I need to see concrete evidence of a turnaround in its financial performance and a clear shift in its technical stage before considering any active engagement.
The missing current fiscal year revenue and order book data are significant red flags that prevent me from assessing its immediate financial momentum. I will be closely monitoring the release of its upcoming financial results for signs of robust growth, improved margins, and successful execution of its ambitious expansion plans, both domestically and internationally. Until then, it's a fascinating watch from the sidelines.
