This report scores a company across three pillars — Fundamentals, Network, and Technicals — and combines them into a single composite score. Here is what each section tells you and what to look for:

1
Header — Composite Score & Badges
The large number top-right is the composite score (0–10). Above 7 = strong candidate; 5–7 = watchlist; below 5 = avoid. Badges tell you at a glance: NN Clean = no governance red flags; Stable Conviction = smart money is holding and not selling; Stage 2 = stock is in an advancing uptrend (Minervini framework); TT 8/8 = perfect Trend Template score (all 8 conditions met).
2
Business Model — What Does the Company Actually Do?
Look at the competitive moat (technology, brand, regulatory licence = durable advantage), key customers (government or blue-chip customers = sticky revenue), and Government Proximity (High = structural tailwind from policy or procurement). Also check debtor days — above 90 days is a yellow flag on cash collection.
3
KYC — Who Owns It, Who Runs It, Who Is Buying It?
Contagion score: director network links to other companies with fraud or regulatory issues. Lower is cleaner — 0–2 is fine, 7+ is a concern. Promoter activity: are insiders buying or selling? Selling = caution. Smart Money grid: shows which quality funds (Nalanda, First Sentier, Norges) hold the stock — and whether they are entering, stable, or exiting over the last 5 quarters. Green blocks = held that quarter. More consecutive green = stronger conviction.
4
Financials — Is the Business Actually Good?
Three sub-tables: Profitability (revenue growth + OPM margin + PAT — look for expanding margins and consistent profit growth), Cash Management (CFO = actual cash generated; CCC = how fast cash is recovered — lower is better), Balance Sheet (D/E below 0.5× = safe; near zero = debt-free). Colored takeaway lines below each table: green = positive signal, amber = watch, red = concern. The peer comparison table shows how this company stacks up on PE and ROCE vs sector competitors.
5
Technical Analysis — Is the Price Set Up to Move?
Stage 2 means the stock is in a confirmed uptrend (price above all moving averages, 200 DMA rising). The Trend Template score (TT) counts how many of 8 Minervini conditions are met — 8/8 is ideal. The price chart shows 1 year of price with 50/150/200 day moving averages and volume. Look for the stock to be sitting above all three moving averages and within 25% of its 52-week high — that is the sweet spot for entry.
₹
Price Targets — How Are They Derived?
Targets are mechanical, not analyst forecasts. If the stock is below its 52-week high by more than 3%: 3M target = CMP + 40% of the gap to 52w high, 6M target = the 52-week high itself, 12M target = 52w high × 1.15. If the stock is already near its high, targets are set at ×1.05 / ×1.12 / ×1.25. The strategy is to exit at 15–20% gain and rotate — not to hold forever.
