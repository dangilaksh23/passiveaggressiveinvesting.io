03Why It's Stage 4 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data.

Sagility is currently in a clear Stage 4, meaning its price is in a long-term downtrend, confirmed by its low TT score of 2/8 and a 1-year return of a mere 2.29% while the broader market has been stronger. I believe this decline is likely influenced by the combined effect of promoter selling activity and, crucially, the market's reaction to the lack of transparent, current financial reporting, specifically the "₹None Cr" for both revenue and order book. In an environment where transparency is key, such data gaps can lead to investor uncertainty and a decline in sentiment, irrespective of underlying strategic plans or sector tailwinds. General market rotation away from certain small-cap IT services or a re-evaluation of valuation multiples could also contribute.

What Would Move This to My Active List

  • I would need to see a clear and positive turnaround in reported financials, with robust revenue growth and a strong order book, moving definitively beyond the "₹None Cr" reported.
  • A cessation of promoter selling, ideally accompanied by promoter buying, would signal renewed conviction from insiders.
  • The technical chart would need to transition out of Stage 4, showing signs of bottoming and beginning to build a Stage 1 base of consolidation.
  • Concrete steps or results demonstrating diversification of client concentration, reducing reliance on the top few customers.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • Continued lack of transparent financial reporting or sustained negative financial performance.
  • Further aggressive promoter selling, indicating a deeper lack of confidence from insiders.
  • Any significant governance issues emerging, or adverse findings by regulatory bodies like SEBI.
  • A structural demand collapse or competitive disruption in their niche that fundamentally erodes their business model.
04Where I Stand Today
Personal Stance
I am not currently holding a position in Sagility India Limited. My stance is firmly on the sidelines, observing this company from a distance. The Stage 4 technical setup is a clear signal to avoid deployment, and the significant lack of current financial data (revenue and order book reported as "₹None Cr") is a major red flag that prevents any active interest at this time.
