02The Financial Picture

Looking at the financial picture, NTES generated a substantial $112.6 billion in revenue for FY2025, marking a respectable 7.0% year-over-year growth from $105.3 billion. While its 5.3% revenue CAGR isn't exactly hyper-growth, it demonstrates consistent expansion for a company of this scale. What truly stands out to me is NTES's exceptional profitability. Its Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin of 43.2% places it in the 96th percentile within its sector, and this trend is improving, which is a powerful signal of efficiency and financial health. Similarly, the Operating Margin of 31.8% is outstanding, ranking in the 94th percentile, indicating strong operational leverage.

The company's ability to convert revenue into cash is clearly a core strength. With an improving FCF trend, NTES is generating significant cash. From a balance sheet perspective, the situation is incredibly robust: a Net Debt/EBITDA of -1.06x indicates the company holds a significant net cash position, providing substantial financial flexibility and resilience. This strong financial foundation, combined with an impressive Return on Equity (ROE) of 21.1% (82nd percentile), paints a picture of a very well-managed and capital-efficient business.

However, I do note a deteriorating trend in its Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) days, which, at 17.8 days, places it in the 28th percentile for its sector. While not a red flag in isolation given the overall financial strength, it's something I'd want to monitor to ensure operational efficiency isn't slipping in this area.
