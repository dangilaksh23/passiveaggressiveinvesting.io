02The Financial Picture

PAC's financial performance in FY2025 painted a robust picture. The company delivered impressive revenue growth, with sales reaching $41.4 billion, a substantial 23.2% increase year-over-year from $33.6 billion. This top-line expansion is further underscored by a healthy 14.8% revenue CAGR, demonstrating consistent demand for its airport services.

Profitability metrics are exceptional, placing PAC at the top of its sector. Its Operating Margin of 42.2% ranks in the 97th percentile, indicating highly efficient operations. Similarly, the Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin of 14.1% (80th percentile) and an outstanding Return on Equity (ROE) of 42.6% (95th percentile) highlight its ability to generate significant cash and returns for shareholders. The balance sheet appears healthy with a Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of 1.92x, which is manageable for an infrastructure-heavy business like an airport operator.

However, a closer look at trends reveals some areas requiring vigilance. While FCF trend is improving, which is excellent, both the Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) trend and the ROE trend are deteriorating. Although the current CCC is a fantastic -56.2 days (97.6th percentile), a deteriorating trend could signal future working capital pressures. Similarly, a declining ROE trend, even from a high base, warrants investigation into its underlying causes to ensure long-term capital efficiency is maintained.

⚠️
I'm noting the deteriorating trends in Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) and Return on Equity (ROE) despite strong current figures. This requires ongoing monitoring for potential fundamental shifts.
03Why It's Stage 3 with a Topping Signal — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

PAC is currently in Stage 3 according to my framework, which typically signifies a period of institutional accumulation. However, the accompanying "topping" signal and a 1-year return of -4.3% suggest that while there might be underlying demand, the stock's price action is showing signs of losing momentum or nearing a peak. This could be due to broader market conditions, sector rotation, or investors re-evaluating growth prospects despite the strong fundamentals. The technical score of 2/8 further reinforces this cautious outlook, indicating a lack of strong bullish momentum.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • A clear technical breakout from this topping pattern, confirming renewed upward momentum and a sustained Stage 2 or strong Stage 3 advance.
  • A reversal in the deteriorating trends for both the Cash Conversion Cycle and Return on Equity, reinforcing the underlying fundamental strength of the business.
  • Continued and increased accumulation by elite funds, signaling strong conviction in PAC's long-term prospects despite short-term technical headwinds.
  • Clarity regarding the company's long-term capital allocation plans and how it intends to sustain its high ROE in the face of its recent deteriorating trend.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A confirmed breakdown into a sustained Stage 4 downtrend, especially if accompanied by a significant deterioration in its core financial metrics.
  • A sustained decline in operating or FCF margins, indicating increased competitive pressure, regulatory changes, or a structural shift in its business model.
