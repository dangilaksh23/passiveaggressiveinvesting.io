This disparity between fundamental strength and market valuation is precisely what captures my attention.

03Why It's Stage 4 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

Trip.com Group is currently in Stage 4, characterized by a downtrend in its price action, as evidenced by its -22.1% return over the past year and a TT Score of 0/8, leading to an "avoid" signal from my technical screen. This Stage 4 designation indicates that the stock is trading below its key moving averages, reflecting sustained selling pressure. While I don't have specific details on the drivers, this could be due to broader sector rotation out of travel, general macroeconomic concerns impacting global tourism, or possibly sentiment dampened by news such as the reported class action lawsuit. It's crucial to distinguish this price action from the underlying business quality, which remains exceptionally strong.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • I would look for clear signs of price stabilization, potentially forming higher lows and breaking above its short-term moving averages, signaling a potential shift out of Stage 4.
  • Continued robust financial performance, particularly sustained revenue growth and stable or expanding margins, would reinforce my confidence in the business's fundamental strength.
  • Positive developments in the global travel industry, such as sustained increases in international travel or reduced geopolitical uncertainties, could provide a tailwind.
  • Resolution or positive updates regarding the mentioned class action lawsuit could remove an overhang on investor sentiment.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A significant and sustained deterioration in Trip.com Group's core financial metrics, such as a sharp decline in FCF margin or a prolonged contraction in revenue growth.
  • Evidence of serious governance issues, significant changes in executive leadership without a clear succession plan, or widespread insider selling.
  • A structural shift in the travel services industry that fundamentally undermines Trip.com Group's competitive advantage or long-term growth prospects.
  • If the company were to take on significant debt, moving away from its current net cash position, without a clear strategic justification.
04Where I Stand Today
