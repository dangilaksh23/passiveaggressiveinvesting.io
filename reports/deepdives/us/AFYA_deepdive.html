02The Financial Picture

Afya's financial performance in FY2025 paints a picture of a rapidly growing and highly profitable business. Revenue hit $3697.3M, marking an impressive 11.9% year-over-year growth and a 16.6% CAGR, indicating consistent expansion. What truly stands out are the company's margins; its Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin of 31.6% places it in the 98.9th percentile relative to its sector, and its Operating Margin of 32.8% is equally stellar, ranking in the 95.7th percentile. These figures demonstrate exceptional operational efficiency and a powerful ability to convert revenue into cash, with the FCF trend itself improving.

Return on Equity (ROE) at 15.5% is solid, sitting comfortably in the 60.9th percentile, reflecting good capital utilization. Cash conversion cycle (CCC) days are a lean 5.7, a strong indicator of efficient working capital management, ranking in the 75.3rd percentile. However, I've noted a deteriorating trend in CCC, which is something I'll keep an eye on, though it starts from a very strong base. The balance sheet appears healthy with a Net Debt/EBITDA of just 1.19x, providing financial flexibility. From a valuation perspective, a trailing PE of 9.1 and Price/Book of 1.43 seem quite attractive for a company exhibiting such strong growth and profitability.

03Why This Stage 2 Momentum Interests Me
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

Afya is currently signaling Stage 2, which suggests a period of price accumulation and upward momentum after a prior base or downtrend. While the 1-year return is negative at -13.9%, the Stage 2 signal indicates that the recent price action has shifted positively, with the stock likely trading above its key moving averages and showing increasing demand. This often implies a recent shift in investor sentiment or a recognition of its underlying value and strong fundamentals, potentially fueling a new uptrend after a period of consolidation or decline.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • Continued confirmation of Stage 2 price action, with increasing volume on up days and strong support at key moving averages.
  • Sustained revenue growth above the 15% mark, demonstrating the scalability of its high-margin business model.
  • A reversal in the deteriorating CCC trend, or at least stabilization, confirming efficient working capital management.
  • Increased transparency regarding customer concentration and the proportion of recurring revenue, reducing business risk unknowns.
  • A shift in insider sentiment towards buying, aligning with the smart money accumulation signal.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A significant and sustained deterioration in FCF or Operating Margins, suggesting a structural challenge to its profitability.
  • A confirmed shift back to Stage 4 price action, indicating a breakdown in the technical setup and renewed selling pressure.
  • Material negative news concerning governance, regulatory changes in Brazil impacting the education sector, or a significant management shake-up.
  • Further, substantial insider selling without any offsetting buys, especially if coupled with a decline in institutional interest.
  • Evidence of significant competitive pressure or a shift in demand that threatens its market position.
