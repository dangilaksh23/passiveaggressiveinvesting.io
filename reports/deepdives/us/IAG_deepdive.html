02The Financial Picture

IAG's financial performance in the most recent fiscal year (2025) is nothing short of impressive. The company reported revenue of $2852.8M, a substantial 74.7% increase year-over-year from $1633.0M. This exceptional growth is further underscored by a robust 3-year Revenue CAGR of 43.8%, demonstrating strong top-line momentum that places it among the top performers in its sector for growth.

Beyond revenue, IAG exhibits excellent profitability and efficiency. Its FCF Margin stands at a healthy 29.5%, ranking in the 94.9th percentile relative to its sector peers, and importantly, this trend is improving. Operating Margin is also very strong at 38.9% (87.9th percentile), indicating effective cost management and operational leverage. Return on Equity (ROE) at 15.8% is solid and also shows an improving trend, reflecting efficient use of shareholder capital. The balance sheet appears robust, with a very low Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of 0.23x, suggesting minimal leverage and strong financial health.

From a valuation perspective, IAG's trailing PE of 9.13 and Price/Book of 2.09 seem quite reasonable, especially when considering its high growth rates and strong profitability margins. These metrics suggest that the market might not be fully appreciating the company's fundamental strength, or it could be factoring in the cyclical nature of the gold industry.

03Why It's Stage 3 — And What Would Change My View
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

IAG is currently in Stage 3, with a "topping" signal, after an incredible 109.7% return over the past year. This stage typically indicates that the stock has experienced a significant advance and is now undergoing distribution or consolidation. While the fundamentals remain strong, the technicals suggest that the momentum that drove its recent surge may be slowing, and the price could be forming a plateau before a potential correction or a prolonged period of sideways movement. This isn't a reflection of business quality, which remains high, but rather a warning to be cautious about new entries at this juncture.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • A clear breakout from its current consolidation, establishing a new Stage 2 uptrend with strong volume, indicating renewed institutional accumulation.
  • Continued reporting of exceptional revenue growth and FCF margin expansion in subsequent quarters, confirming the sustainability of its operational performance.
  • Positive developments in its mining operations, such as significant new resource discoveries or efficiency improvements leading to increased production guidance.
  • A shift in insider sentiment from selling to buying, or a substantial increase in elite fund accumulation, signaling strong conviction from informed players.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A sustained and significant decline in gold prices that materially impacts IAG's revenue and profitability, eroding its strong margins.
  • Evidence of operational missteps or unexpected increases in production costs that lead to a consistent deterioration in FCF margins or revenue growth.
  • A substantial increase in the company's debt levels or other balance sheet weakening without a clear strategic rationale.
  • The emergence of negative news regarding environmental, social, or governance (ESG) issues, or a widespread and unexplained insider liquidation.
04Where I Stand Today
Personal Stance
IAG's fundamental quality and explosive growth are genuinely compelling, making it a business I want to keep on my radar. Its high Quality Score, impressive FCF generation, and strong balance sheet are all characteristics I seek in long-term investments. However, the current Stage 3 topping signal, following such an impressive 12-month run, gives me pause. I believe it's prudent to watch from the sidelines for now.
My current stance is to observe how IAG's price action develops over the coming weeks and months. I'm looking for a clear resolution to this topping pattern—either a healthy consolidation that eventually leads to a new Stage 2 uptrend, or a deeper correction that could present a more attractive entry point if the fundamentals remain intact. Given its high quality, my patience horizon for this type of opportunity can stretch several quarters, as I prioritize entering when both technicals and fundamentals align for sustainable upside.
