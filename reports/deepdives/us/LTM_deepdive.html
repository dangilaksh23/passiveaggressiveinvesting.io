Institutional Backing

Despite the low institutional presence, I'm noting a "smart money accumulating" signal, with firms like Robeco, Jane Street, Citadel, and Two Sigma adding positions this quarter. This suggests some sophisticated investors are finding value here, even if it's not yet widespread.

02The Financial Picture

LTM has demonstrated impressive top-line growth, with revenue reaching $14.27 billion in FY25, an 11.2% increase year-over-year, and a robust 15.1% CAGR. This indicates strong demand for its services within the Industrials/Airlines sector. The business is also highly efficient, evidenced by an operating margin of 16.4% and a free cash flow (FCF) margin of 13.0%, both ranking well above the sector average. Crucially, its FCF trend is improving, suggesting growing financial health and operational leverage.

The company's cash conversion cycle (CCC) of -14.0 days is exceptional, ranking in the 92nd percentile, and is also showing an improving trend. This negative CCC implies LTM is receiving cash from customers before paying its suppliers, a very strong indicator of working capital efficiency. Return on Equity (ROE) is an outstanding 108.5%, placing it in the 99th percentile for its sector. However, it's critical to note that the ROE trend is deteriorating, which I need to monitor closely. The balance sheet appears manageable with a Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of 2.65x, which is reasonable for an airline. Valuation metrics show a trailing PE of 8.35 and Price/Book of 8.045.

⚠️
While LTM's ROE is sector-leading, the reported deteriorating trend warrants close attention. I need to understand the underlying causes to ensure this doesn't signal a structural issue rather than a temporary fluctuation.
03Why It's Stage 3 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

LTM is currently in Stage 3, characterized by a "topping" signal after a significant 22.2% return over the past year. This technical stage suggests that the stock has experienced a strong run and is now potentially consolidating or showing early signs of a reversal. While the underlying business fundamentals remain robust, the price action indicates increased supply or a pause in upward momentum. This doesn't automatically disqualify the business from my watchlist, especially given its high quality score, but it prompts caution regarding immediate entry.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • Clear stabilization or reversal of the deteriorating ROE trend, ideally with management commentary explaining the previous decline.
  • A move into Stage 2 (advancing phase) on the technical charts, confirming renewed upward momentum and demand.
  • Continued acceleration in revenue growth coupled with sustained or expanding FCF margins.
  • Further accumulation by elite funds and a notable increase in overall institutional ownership, signalling growing conviction.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A sustained decline into Stage 4 (downtrend) without a clear fundamental reason or a significant deterioration of key financial metrics.
  • Any signs of significant governance issues, particularly given the lack of board information in my current data.
  • A material increase in Net Debt/EBITDA, especially if coupled with a contraction in FCF.
  • Evidence of structural demand destruction for its services or increasing competitive pressures that erode its strong margins.
