02The Financial Picture

BLBD has demonstrated impressive growth, with revenue increasing by 9.9% year-over-year to $1.48 billion in FY2025, and a robust 3-year revenue CAGR of 22.7%. This indicates strong and sustained demand for their specialized transportation solutions, allowing them to expand their top line effectively.

The company also boasts healthy profitability and efficiency. Operating margins stand at 11.3% and are improving, while the FCF margin of 10.4% is solid and trending positively. Their outstanding Return on Equity (ROE) of 50.0%, placing them in the 96th percentile for the sector, highlights highly efficient use of shareholder capital. Furthermore, BLBD's balance sheet is exceptionally strong, reflected in a net debt/EBITDA of -0.72x, indicating a net cash position. Their cash conversion cycle (CCC Days) of just 1.4 is incredibly efficient and also trending in the right direction.

03Why This Stage 2 Momentum Interests Me
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

BLBD is currently in a Stage 2 technical setup, which indicates a strong price uptrend and positive momentum. This is further supported by a remarkable 1-year return of 73.2% and a "buy_zone" signal from my framework. The company's exceptional fundamentals and high quality score are clearly aligning with positive price action, suggesting sustained investor interest in its specialized market. This combination of fundamental strength and technical momentum is what makes it intriguing for my watchlist.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • Clear evidence of improved corporate governance, specifically the addition of independent directors to the board.
  • Continued robust revenue growth that consistently outpaces sector averages, demonstrating sustained market leadership.
  • A sustained expansion of profitability margins, particularly FCF and Operating Margins, signaling increased operational leverage.
  • A noticeable shift in insider sentiment, moving from net selling to net buying, which would align insider incentives with shareholder interests.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A sustained breakdown below key moving averages, signaling a shift to Stage 3 or Stage 4, which would invalidate the current momentum thesis.
  • A significant and persistent deterioration in financial performance, such as a sharp decline in FCF margin or a significant slowdown in revenue growth.
  • An acceleration of insider selling without a compelling and transparent justification.
  • Any material negative news regarding regulatory issues, significant product recalls, or a structural decline in demand for their core products.
04Where I Stand Today
Personal Stance
I'm currently watching BLBD from the sidelines, intrigued by its strong financial quality, impressive growth trajectory, and its current Stage 2 momentum. The company's leadership position in the essential school and commercial bus markets provides a compelling long-term narrative for consistent demand.
While the technical picture is strong and the financials are excellent, the lack of independent board members and the recent insider selling are notable concerns that I need to monitor closely. I would ideally like to see some tangible improvements in governance or a more neutral insider sentiment before considering a deeper dive or potential position.
My patience horizon for this opportunity is medium-term, as I'm looking for continued execution on the financial front alongside potential improvements in governance that would solidify my conviction and reduce some of the identified risks.
