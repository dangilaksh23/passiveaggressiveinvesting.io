02The Financial Picture

Looking at the most recent fiscal year (2025), GPAEF demonstrates robust financial health. Revenue grew by an impressive 23.2% year-over-year to $41,408.5 million, contributing to a strong 14.8% Revenue CAGR. This indicates significant demand for its services and effective operational execution. The company's profitability metrics are exceptional, with an Operating Margin of 42.2% (ranking in the 97th percentile within its sector) and a Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin of 14.1% (80th percentile). The FCF trend is also improving, which I view as a strong indicator of increasing financial strength and flexibility.

Balance sheet health appears solid, with a Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of 1.92x. This is a reasonable leverage level for an infrastructure-heavy business that often relies on debt financing for capital-intensive projects. Cash conversion is outstanding, reflected in a CCC Days of -56.2, ranking in the 98th percentile. While the CCC trend is noted as deteriorating and ROE trend is also deteriorating, their current levels are still extremely strong, suggesting a potential normalization from exceptionally high efficiency or specific operational shifts rather than an immediate concern. I'll be watching these trends closely for any sustained downward movement.
