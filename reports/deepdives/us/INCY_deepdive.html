Business Model · 10-K 2026-02

Incyte is a global biopharmaceutical company focused on discovering, developing, and commercializing novel therapeutics. The company operates in three key therapeutic areas: Hematology, Oncology, and Inflammation and Autoimmunity. Incyte generates revenue through the sale of its approved medicines, such as JAKAFI, ICLUSIG, MONJUVI/MINJUVI, and NIKTIMVO, and also earns milestones and royalties from collaborations. Its competitive distinction lies in its proprietary drug discovery and development capabilities, particularly in targeting rare blood cancers and inflammatory diseases.

Key Risks
△ Intense competition from larger pharmaceutical companies with greater resources may hinder Incyte's ability to develop and commercialize products.
△ The lengthy, expensive, and uncertain regulatory review and approval process by agencies like the FDA poses a significant risk to product launches.
△ Inadequate protection of proprietary information through trade secrets and confidentiality agreements could lead to competitors developing similar technologies.
△ Governmental pressure in some countries to lower drug prices or grant compulsory licenses could adversely affect sales and operations.
△ The success of Incyte's business depends on its ability to develop proprietary products, obtain patent protection, and secure regulatory approvals.
