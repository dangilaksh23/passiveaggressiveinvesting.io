02The Financial Picture

NUTX's financial performance is exceptionally strong, marked by explosive revenue growth. In fiscal year 2025, revenue surged by an impressive 82.4% year-over-year to $875.3M, contributing to a remarkable 58.6% Revenue CAGR. This indicates a business rapidly scaling its operations and capturing market share effectively within its diverse healthcare segments.

Profitability metrics are equally compelling; the company boasts a sector-leading FCF Margin of 28.1% and an Operating Margin of 31.5%, both ranking in the top decile relative to peers. These robust margins, coupled with an improving FCF and ROE trend, demonstrate excellent operational leverage and capital efficiency. The balance sheet appears healthy, with a conservative Net Debt/EBITDA of 0.58x, suggesting ample financial flexibility and capacity for continued expansion.

While the overall financial picture is robust, I note a deteriorating trend in Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) days, currently at 96.6 days and ranking in the 55.7th percentile. Although this isn't dire, a worsening trend could indicate potential future pressures on working capital efficiency if not managed proactively. However, this is a minor point against the backdrop of otherwise stellar financial performance and growth.

03Why This Stage 2 Momentum Interests Me
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

NUTX is currently in a Stage 2 technical setup, which indicates that its price is in an uptrend, typically above key moving averages, following a period of consolidation. The 'weak_stage2' signal suggests that while the uptrend is present, it might lack the strong conviction or volume characteristic of a robust Stage 2 breakout. This momentum is likely fueled by the company's exceptional financial performance, particularly its explosive revenue growth and strong profitability, which are attracting investor interest despite the broader market's cautious sentiment towards healthcare.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • I would look for a strengthening of the Stage 2 signal, evidenced by increased volume and price conviction, confirming broader market participation in the uptrend.
  • Continued robust revenue growth, maintaining or exceeding current CAGR, alongside further margin expansion would significantly reinforce my conviction.
  • A clear improvement in the Cash Conversion Cycle trend, signaling enhanced operational efficiency in working capital management.
  • Strategic announcements or execution demonstrating successful expansion into new, high-growth healthcare service lines or geographies.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A significant and sustained decline in FCF margin or operating margin, indicating a fundamental deterioration in profitability.
  • A reversal in insider sentiment, particularly if key executives begin to sell shares en masse, or substantial liquidation by elite funds.
  • The emergence of negative news, regulatory headwinds, or unforeseen competitive pressures specific to NUTX that could structurally impair its business model.
  • A clear breakdown into a sustained Stage 4 downtrend without underlying quality justification, suggesting a loss
