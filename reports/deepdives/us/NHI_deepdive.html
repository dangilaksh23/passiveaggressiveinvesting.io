Business Model · 10-K 2026-02

National Health Investors, Inc. is a real estate investment trust (REIT) that invests in healthcare and senior living properties. The company generates revenue primarily through rental income from its portfolio of healthcare facilities, which are leased to various operators and tenants. Its competitive distinctiveness lies in its specialized focus on the healthcare real estate sector and its established relationships within the industry.

Key Risks
△ Deterioration in the financial condition or business prospects of tenants, managers, and borrowers can adversely affect the company.
△ A significant percentage of rental income comes from a small number of tenants, making the failure of any one of them impactful.
△ The company is exposed to risks associated with government regulations and payors like Medicare and Medicaid, and changes to reimbursement rates.
△ Pandemics, epidemics, or outbreaks can materially adversely affect the businesses and results of operations of its operators.
△ The illiquidity of real estate investments could hinder the company's ability to respond to adverse property performance.
