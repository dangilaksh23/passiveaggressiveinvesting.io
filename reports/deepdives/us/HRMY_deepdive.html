02The Financial Picture

HRMY demonstrates impressive financial strength, particularly in its revenue growth and cash generation. For the most recent fiscal year (2025), revenue hit $868.5 million, a healthy 21.5% increase year-over-year, contributing to a robust 25.6% revenue CAGR. This indicates a business with strong market demand and effective growth strategies.

The company's profitability and cash efficiency are exceptional. Its Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin stands at an outstanding 40.1%, placing it in the 93.9th percentile relative to its sector peers, and this trend is improving. The Operating Margin of 24.0% also ranks highly at the 78.2nd percentile. These figures highlight HRMY's ability to convert sales into significant cash, which is a hallmark of a high-quality business. The balance sheet is also very healthy, with a Net Debt/EBITDA of -2.31x, indicating a net cash position.

While the Return on Equity (ROE) at 18.2% is respectable and ranks in the 70.9th percentile, I'm concerned by the fact that the ROE trend is deteriorating. This could signal increasing capital intensity or pressure on profitability that isn't yet fully reflected in the FCF or operating margins. Coupled with a trailing PE of 13.64 for a growth company with such strong FCF, it suggests the market is pricing in some level of risk or uncertainty.

⚠️
A deteriorating ROE trend, coupled with zero independent directors on a 16-person board and an extreme CEO pay ratio of 1990:1, raises serious governance concerns. These internal issues could be contributing to the stock's Stage 4 technical weakness and the persistent insider selling.
03Why It's Stage 4 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

HRMY is currently in a Stage 4 downtrend, reflected by its -9.0% 1-year return and a "signal: avoid" from my technical scoring system. This means its price is trading below key moving averages, indicating significant technical weakness. Given the strong fundamental quality of the business — excellent revenue growth, high FCF margins, and a healthy balance sheet — this price decline isn't necessarily a reflection of operational failure. Instead, it could be driven by broader sector headwinds in biotechnology, general market pessimism, or, more likely, a negative market reaction to the significant governance issues and consistent insider selling I've observed.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • I would need to see concrete steps towards improved corporate governance, such as the appointment of independent directors to the board, which currently has zero.
  • A reversal in the deteriorating ROE trend, indicating that the business can sustain its high profitability and efficient use of equity.
  • A shift in insider sentiment, specifically insider buying activity, which would signal renewed confidence from those closest to the company.
  • A technical shift to Stage 1 or 2, indicating that the price downtrend has stabilized and a new accumulation phase is beginning, supported by fundamental improvements.
