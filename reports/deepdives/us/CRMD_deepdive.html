Business Quality Signals
CRMD operates in critical end markets like hemodialysis, hospitals, and emergency departments, providing essential products within the hospital ecosystem. Their key customers include large and mid-sized dialysis organizations, suggesting a robust B2B model. The business benefits from recurring contracts and reports no supplier risk, which are strong indicators of a stable and predictable revenue stream.
02The Financial Picture

CRMD's recent financial performance is nothing short of explosive. In FY2025, revenue surged to $311.7M, marking an astounding 616.6% year-over-year growth from $43.5M. This dramatic expansion is flowing directly to the bottom line, evidenced by an exceptional Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin of 55.4% and an Operating Margin of 48.2%. Both metrics rank in the top percentiles relative to its sector peers, indicating a highly efficient and profitable operation. This strong profitability is also reflected in an impressive Return on Equity (ROE) of 40.2%, which is also among the best in its sector.

The FCF and ROE trends are both improving, suggesting that the company is not only growing rapidly but also becoming more financially robust. The balance sheet appears remarkably clean, with a Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of just 0.02x, indicating minimal leverage. This financial strength provides a solid foundation for continued growth and resilience.

⚠️
I'm noting a significant concern regarding CRMD's Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) Days, which stand at a very high 422.1 and are deteriorating. While profitability is excellent, this suggests potential issues with working capital management or extended payment terms that could tie up cash. This is a crucial area I'd need to see improvement in for long-term confidence.
03Why This Stage 2 Momentum Interests Me
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

CRMD is currently signaling a 1-to-2 transition, which is precisely what I look for in identifying emerging momentum opportunities. After a challenging year where the stock saw a -38.9% return, indicating a probable Stage 4 decline or extended Stage 1 base, the technical picture is now shifting. A Stage 1-to-2 transition suggests the stock is moving out of a consolidation phase and beginning a new uptrend. The TT Score of 2/8 confirms this is early-stage momentum, meaning there's potential for significant upside if the trend solidifies. My interest here is in catching the early innings of what could be a sustained move, driven by the underlying fundamental strength finally being recognized by the market.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • I would look for sustained revenue growth rates, ideally maintaining above 50% year-over-year, demonstrating continued market penetration and demand for their products.
  • A noticeable improvement in the Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) Days, showing better working capital management and translating exceptional FCF margins into actual cash faster.
  • A shift in insider sentiment, with new insider buys outweighing sells, or a significant reduction in insider selling activity, aligning management's interests more closely with shareholders.
  • Confirmation of the Stage 2 uptrend with increased volume and consistent price action above key moving averages, signaling strong institutional support for the new trend.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • Any significant and sustained decline in FCF or Operating Margins, suggesting a loss of pricing power or increased operational costs that erode its core profitability.
  • A return to Stage 4 price action, indicating the momentum has failed and the stock is reverting to a downtrend, signaling that my thesis for an emerging uptrend was premature or incorrect.
  • Negative news regarding product efficacy, regulatory hurdles, or unexpected competition that could structurally impact its end markets or customer relationships.
  • Further deterioration in the Cash Conversion Cycle or substantial increase in debt, indicating financial strain despite its current strong balance sheet.
04Where I Stand Today
Personal Stance
I am currently watching CRMD from the sidelines. The combination of explosive revenue growth, sector-leading profitability, and a clean balance sheet is highly attractive, warranting its "High Quality" score. The technical signal of a 1-to-2 transition aligns with my strategy of identifying businesses with strong fundamentals that are beginning new uptrends.
However, I have a few hesitations that keep me from acting immediately. The high and deteriorating Cash Conversion Cycle is a notable concern, and the ongoing insider selling, despite strong smart money accumulation, makes me cautious. I'd like to see further confirmation of the Stage 2 momentum, ideally coupled with some positive developments around working capital management or a shift in insider behavior. My patience horizon for this type of opportunity is medium-term; I'm willing to wait for a clearer signal that these concerns are either being addressed or are not material enough to derail the fundamental strength and technical uptrend.
