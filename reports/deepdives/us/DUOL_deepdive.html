Business Model · 10-K 2026-02

Duolingo is a technology company that offers a mobile-first, gamified platform for learning languages, math, music, and chess. Its mission is to make high-quality education universally accessible, leveraging smartphone technology to deliver bite-sized, engaging lessons. The company generates revenue through a freemium model, offering free access to its core content with ads, and paid subscriptions for an ad-free experience and additional features. Duolingo's competitive advantage lies in its massive user engagement data, AI-powered personalization, and a strong brand that has become synonymous with language learning.

Key Risks
△ Failure to retain existing users or attract new users, or a decrease in user engagement, could significantly harm revenue and financial results.
△ The highly competitive online language learning industry, with low switching costs and new entrants, could disrupt the business.
△ Changes in user behavior, including decreased frequency or duration of product use, could negatively impact engagement and monetization.
△ Users may become unwilling to pay for subscriptions or in-app purchases, or the company may be unable to increase prices.
