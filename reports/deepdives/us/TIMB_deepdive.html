02The Financial Picture

TIMB presents a compelling financial profile, particularly for a telecom operator. In its most recent fiscal year (2025), the company reported revenues of $26.6 billion, representing a solid 4.6% year-over-year growth and a respectable 7.3% revenue CAGR. This consistent growth provides a stable foundation, but it's the profitability and cash generation that truly stand out.

The business demonstrates exceptional operating leverage and cash conversion. Its Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin is an impressive 33.4%, placing it in the 93.8th percentile relative to its sector peers, and this trend is improving. Operating Margin is also robust at 25.0%, ranking in the 87.8th percentile. Return on Equity (ROE) at 18.0% (76.4th percentile) with an improving trend further underscores its capital efficiency. Moreover, the negative CCC Days of -75.1 (75.7th percentile) indicate excellent working capital management, essentially getting paid before paying its suppliers, and this trend is stable. The balance sheet appears healthy with a low Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of 0.93x, suggesting conservative leverage. Valuations like a Trailing PE of 12.4x seem reasonable given the strong profitability.

⚠️
I'm noting significant gaps in the reported data, specifically the 'None' for employees and '0' for board size, alongside missing details on end markets and customers. This lack of transparency makes a full fundamental assessment challenging and introduces an element of uncertainty.
03Why It's Stage 3 — And What Would Change That
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 3 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

TIMB is currently in Stage 3, indicating a topping pattern after a strong run that saw a 20.2% return over the past year. My TT Score for this name is 4/8, reinforcing the signal that momentum is stalling or potentially reversing. This doesn't necessarily mean the business fundamentals are deteriorating; rather, it suggests that the price has likely run up and is now consolidating or experiencing distribution. For a high-quality business like TIMB, a topping signal might be driven by broader sector rotation, profit-taking after its strong performance, or perhaps specific macro pressures in the Brazilian market.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • A clear resolution of the topping pattern, leading to a constructive base formation and a breakout into a new Stage 2 uptrend with increased volume.
  • Continued improvement in revenue growth momentum, ideally pushing the CAGR higher while maintaining or expanding current impressive margins.
  • Enhanced transparency from the company, providing more detailed information on its operational structure, customer base, and governance (e.g., employee count, board composition).
  • Sustained accumulation by institutional investors, particularly if the percentage of institutional ownership begins to rise significantly from its current low levels.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A confirmed breakdown into a Stage 4 downtrend, signaling sustained price weakness that isn't quickly reversed.
  • A material and sustained decline in its exceptional FCF Margin or Operating Margin, indicating fundamental business deterioration.
  • Any signs of significant governance issues or a lack of clarity around the operational data, especially if it impacts financial reporting credibility.
  • A substantial increase in its Net Debt/EBITDA ratio, signaling a shift towards a more leveraged and potentially riskier balance sheet.
