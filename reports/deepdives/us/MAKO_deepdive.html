02The Financial Picture

MAKO's financial performance is exceptionally strong, reflecting robust growth and impressive profitability. For the most recent fiscal year 2025, revenue surged to $148.5 million, marking a substantial 61.2% year-over-year increase from $92.1 million. This growth isn't a one-off; the company boasts a compelling 32.8% revenue CAGR, placing it in the top tier for growth within its sector.

Profitability margins are where MAKO truly shines. Its Free Cash Flow (FCF) Margin stands at an excellent 27.4%, ranking in the 91.9th percentile relative to its sector peers, and importantly, this trend is improving. Similarly, the Operating Margin of 36.5% is in the 84.9th percentile, indicating superior operational efficiency. Return on Equity (ROE) at 22.4% is also very strong and improving, sitting in the 88.4th percentile. These figures collectively paint a picture of a business that effectively converts revenue into profits and shareholder returns.

The balance sheet appears robust, with a Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of -1.21x, signifying a net cash position rather than debt. This provides a strong foundation and flexibility. The primary financial concern I note is the Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) of 77.4 days, which is in the 42.4th percentile and shows a deteriorating trend. While not immediately alarming given the strong FCF, it's an area I would want to see improve, as efficient cash conversion is crucial for long-term health, even for highly profitable businesses.
