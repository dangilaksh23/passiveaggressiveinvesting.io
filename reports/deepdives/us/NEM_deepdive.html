Institutional Backing
I see strong institutional conviction with 81.4% ownership and 38.2% held by elite funds like Blackrock, Vanguard, and State Street. However, my smart money signal shows distribution this quarter, and insider sentiment is decidedly negative with 10 sells and no buys. Van Eck, Geode, FMR, and Invesco were notable funds adding shares, which offers a conflicting signal I need to monitor closely.
Business Quality Signals
Newmont benefits from diversified end markets for gold, spanning jewelry, electronics, dentistry, industrial uses, and investment products. While there's no specific customer concentration to flag, and no recurring contracts or supplier risks noted, the fundamental demand for gold across these sectors provides a solid foundation. The business itself seems robust on the operational front.
02The Financial Picture

From a financial standpoint, Newmont is exceptionally strong. My framework shows revenue growing robustly, jumping from $18.68 billion to $22.67 billion year-over-year, representing a 21.3% increase. The three-year revenue CAGR stands impressively at 23.9%, indicating sustained top-line momentum. This growth is translating directly to the bottom line with an outstanding FCF Margin of 32.2%, ranking in the 96th percentile within its sector, and an Operating Margin of 48.6%, placing it in the 94th percentile. These figures suggest excellent operational efficiency and pricing power.

The company's profitability extends to its return on equity, which is a healthy 20.9%, ranking it in the 84th percentile, and showing an improving trend. Perhaps most compelling is the balance sheet, which boasts a Net Debt/EBITDA ratio of -0.15x, indicating a net cash position—a highly desirable trait in any environment, especially for a capital-intensive industry. The only area of concern I've noted is the cash conversion cycle (CCC Days) at 81.6, which ranks in the lower 38th percentile and shows a deteriorating trend. While not immediately alarming given the overall strength, it's something I'd want to see stabilize or improve over time.

03Why This Stage 3 Consolidation Interests Me — And What Would Change My View
Technical analysis follows the stage framework described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for US equities. Stage 4 means the price is below key moving averages — it reflects price action, not business quality.

Newmont is currently in Stage 3, indicating a period of consolidation or base-building after a significant run, evidenced by its 91.1% one-year return. My technical indicators also show a "topping" signal, suggesting potential profit-taking or a pause in upward momentum. This isn't surprising given the strong performance and could be a natural digestion period for such a large move. I view this as the market attempting to find equilibrium after a rapid ascent, rather than a fundamental issue with the underlying business. The high quality and robust financials make this consolidation particularly interesting as a potential reset, assuming the topping signal resolves constructively.

What Would Upgrade My View

  • I'd look for a clear resolution of the Stage 3 consolidation, ideally with a constructive breakout on increasing volume, confirming renewed demand.
  • A reversal in insider sentiment, with more insider buys than sells, would provide a strong vote of confidence from those closest to the business.
  • Continued strength in gold prices would provide a tailwind, reinforcing the sector's positive outlook and NEM's revenue streams.
  • Evidence of the Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC Days) stabilizing or improving would alleviate my minor concern regarding operational efficiency.

What Would Make Me Stop Watching

  • A sustained breakdown from the current consolidation, entering a clear Stage 4 downtrend, especially if accompanied by heavy volume.
  • Significant and prolonged weakness in gold prices that materially impacts Newmont's profitability and future guidance.
  • Any significant negative news regarding operational disruptions, environmental issues, or a substantial deterioration of its net cash position.
  • Further deterioration in governance, particularly the continued absence of independent board members, could raise long-term concerns for me.
04Where I Stand Today
Personal Stance
Today, I am watching Newmont from the sidelines. The combination of its exceptional financial quality, robust growth, and strong balance sheet makes it a very appealing business. However, the current Stage 3 consolidation with a "topping" signal, coupled with insider selling and smart money distribution, suggests caution is warranted. I respect the technical picture and the signals from those with the most intimate knowledge of the company.
I'm not looking to jump in during a period of uncertainty, even for a high-quality name. My patience horizon for an opportunity like this would typically extend several quarters, allowing the technical picture to clarify. I want to see a clear sign that the market has finished digesting its prior gains and is ready for its next move, ideally supported by a shift in insider and smart money sentiment.
