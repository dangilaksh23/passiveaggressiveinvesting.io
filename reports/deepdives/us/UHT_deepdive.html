Business Model · 10-K 2026-02

Universal Health Realty Income Trust is a real estate investment trust (REIT) that owns and operates healthcare and human service-related facilities. The company generates revenue by leasing these properties, which include acute care hospitals, behavioral health hospitals, medical office buildings, and childcare centers, to healthcare providers and other tenants. Its competitive distinction lies in its specialized focus on healthcare real estate and its long-standing relationships with major healthcare operators.

Key Risks
△ Dependence on a few major tenants, particularly subsidiaries of Universal Health Services, Inc., could impact revenue if these tenants experience financial difficulties or terminate leases.
△ The healthcare industry is subject to extensive government regulation, which could affect the operations and profitability of its tenants and, consequently, the company.
△ The company's real estate investments are concentrated in the healthcare sector, making it vulnerable to industry-specific downturns or changes in healthcare policy.
△ The company's ability to attract and retain tenants depends on the demand for healthcare services and the financial health of healthcare providers.
△ Vacancies in its facilities, such as vacant land or specialty facilities, can lead to reduced rental income and increased carrying costs.
