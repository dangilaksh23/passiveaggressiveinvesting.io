Business Model · 10-K 2026-02

Gladstone Commercial Corporation is a real estate investment trust (REIT) that focuses on acquiring, owning, and managing industrial and office properties. The company generates revenue primarily through rental income from these properties, often under long-term net leases where tenants are responsible for most operating expenses. Gladstone Commercial aims to provide stable cash flow and increase in value by investing in secondary growth markets with favorable economic trends and diversified industries, targeting primarily single-tenant industrial properties.

Key Risks
△ Tenant inability to pay rent due to financial distress, leveraged transactions, or economic downturns could reduce cash available for distributions.
△ Tenant bankruptcy could lead to loss of lease payments, increased property carrying costs, and reduced asset value.
△ Inability to renew leases or re-lease vacant space at favorable rates could negatively impact business operations and distributions.
△ Net leases may not result in fair market rental rates over time due to long lease terms and tenant flexibility, potentially lowering income.
△ Multi-tenant properties face risks from tenant turnover, fluctuating occupancy rates, and difficulty finding suitable replacement tenants.
Key Initiatives
→ Acquire, own, and manage primarily industrial and office properties.
→ Target secondary growth markets with favorable economic trends, diversified industries, and growing population/employment.
→ Focus on single-tenant industrial properties, while potentially acquiring select multi-tenant industrial and office properties.
→ Enter into net leases with terms of approximately seven to 20 years with built-in rental rate increases.
