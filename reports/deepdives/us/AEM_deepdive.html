02The Financial Picture

AEM's financial performance in FY2025 is nothing short of impressive. Revenue surged by 43.7% year-over-year to $11,907.9M, contributing to a robust 27.5% revenue CAGR. This kind of top-line expansion, especially for a Basic Materials company, indicates significant market demand or successful strategic execution.

Profitability metrics are where AEM truly shines. It boasts an exceptional FCF Margin of 35.8% and an Operating Margin of 53.1%, both ranking in the top percentiles (97th and 97.5th respectively) against its sector peers. This suggests a highly efficient and potentially low-cost operation. Both FCF and ROE trends are improving, reinforcing the picture of a business enhancing its core profitability and shareholder returns. The balance sheet is also a fortress, with a Net Debt/EBITDA of -0.3x, meaning AEM holds a net cash position, providing significant financial flexibility.

However, it's not all gold that glitters. My framework highlights a clear area of concern: the Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) stands at 99.8 days, placing it in the 25.5th percentile for the sector, and the trend is deteriorating. While the company generates massive free cash flow, the efficiency with which it converts working capital into cash is lagging. This could indicate issues with inventory management, receivables collection, or payables, and it's something I'll be watching closely.
