  • Multi-segment manufacturer: automotive die casting, industrial machinery, storage equipment. The aluminium die casting business is EV-agnostic — aluminium components are used in ICE and EV powertrains alike.
  • Moderate regulatory exposure — PLI-AUTO is an incentive scheme, not a purchase order. The multi-segment presence (industrial, storage) reduces reliance on any single regulatory anchor, but C1 tops out at moderate for auto.
  • Strong management quality — multi-segment execution is harder than single-segment and CRAFTSMAN has delivered on it. D/E at 0.58x is manageable but higher than peers and worth monitoring through a capex cycle.
  • At scoring CMP ~₹7,000, forward P/E was 47–56x — fair for the quality but not a deep discount. Upside to ₹9,000 (~29%) scored partially on C3 because the valuation discount is not compelling at current levels.
  • The technical base failing to form (price resuming downtrend from ATH ₹8,220), D/E deteriorating as capex continues, or the storage equipment business thesis not materialising as a genuine margin driver.
