  • OFC, telecom equipment, and defence communication manufacturer. BharatNet Phase III is the primary government demand anchor. PAT inflected from losses to ₹97.6 Cr in Q3 FY26 (+32.55% YoY). This was a missed position — I scored it at ₹72 in early April 2026; by April 24 the CMP was ₹98–100, a 36% move in three weeks.
  • BharatNet Phase III (₹1.39 Lakh Cr) is direct government procurement — the sovereign is the buyer, not an OEM intermediary. BSNL and defence add two further government-facing demand streams. The combination means the majority of HFCL's addressable market is sovereign-driven, not merely incentivised. C1=25 maximum.
  • Promoter Mahendra Nahata issued ₹555 Cr of warrants at ₹74 in March 2026 — near the then-market price of ₹72. This is a large capital commitment at fair-market terms, not a dilutive discount. Stage 2 confirmed (TT 6/8). Institutional base is moderate; government-dependency perception caps FII but the promoter signal is the more important C2 component here.
  • At scoring CMP ₹72, HFCL was at ~27x P/E on inflecting PAT with four consecutive revenue growth quarters. That combination produced C3=30 (maximum). The original scoring conditions no longer apply at ₹98–100. The score recorded here is a point-in-time snapshot.
  • BharatNet Phase III disbursements reversing or being reallocated, PAT regression to loss-making quarters, Stage 2 deteriorating to Stage 3 or 4, or the promoter not executing on the ₹555 Cr warrant commitment. Any of these would invalidate one of the three core legs of the thesis.
