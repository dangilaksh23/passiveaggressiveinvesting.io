EIEL executes water and wastewater treatment infrastructure projects. Every rupee of revenue comes from government procurement — JJM (Jal Jeevan Mission), AMRUT 2.0, and the NTPC BESS (Battery Energy Storage) project. This is not a company that sells to consumers or private developers and hopes government policy helps. Government is the buyer. That distinction matters to how I score it.

02
Valuation as I See It

At a scoring CMP of ~₹152, the TTM P/E was around 14x. For an EPC company growing revenue at 45% YoY with 24% EBITDA margins and sub-60-day debtor collection, that struck me as meaningfully undervalued relative to the growth and the quality of the customer base.

The order book of ~₹3,500 Cr provides roughly 2–2.5 years of revenue visibility at current run rates. This is important context: the valuation is not purely based on trailing earnings — there is booked future revenue behind it.

D/E at 0.26x is very low for an EPC business, which typically runs higher leverage to fund working capital. The combination of low leverage, high margins, and government-backed receivables made this one of the stronger valuation cases in my universe at the time of scoring.

I do not publish price targets in these notes. What I can say is that the discount to fair value at scoring CMP was meaningful by the metrics I use (P/E relative to growth rate, upside to my private estimate of intrinsic value).

03
Chart Structure

Some of the chart-structure thinking on this site is informed by the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data and personal use.

EIEL IPO'd in November 2024 at ₹148. It reached an all-time high of ₹391.60 within 18 days of listing — a post-IPO euphoria run. It then corrected 65% to ₹134.71, which happens to sit right at the IPO price.

At scoring (CMP ₹152–213), the stock was forming what I read as a primary base — the initial consolidation after the post-IPO correction. The 50-DMA was at ₹174.70 and the 200-DMA at ₹222.90. Price was below both moving averages, which means it does not pass the Stage 2 Trend Template at scoring.

The C4 score of 8/20 reflects this — primary base gets partial credit in my framework because the fundamentals remain intact and the correction looks like consolidation rather than fundamental deterioration. But a Stage 2 confirmation with the 50-DMA clearing above the 200-DMA (and the stock clearing both) would move the C4 score substantially.

Promoters buying in the ₹138–148 range during the correction is a significant data point here. It tells me the people closest to the business are not reading this as fundamental deterioration either.

04
What Could Change My Mind

Hard invalidators — things that would make me reassess fundamentally:

A sustained slowdown in JJM or AMRUT 2.0 disbursements, particularly if it reflects a policy reprioritisation rather than a temporary delay. The entire C1 thesis rests on government spending actually flowing to these projects.

State-level payment delays extending past 90 days regularly. EPC companies with government customers are structurally exposed to working capital risk when state budgets are squeezed. EIEL's low debtor days are an asset — if that number starts creeping up, it's a warning sign.

The NTPC BESS project being cancelled or materially delayed. This is a specific government-anchored project that adds to the thesis. Its removal would reduce the C1 score.

Soft watch points — things I'm monitoring but not yet acting on:

Promoter selling in the open market. They were buyers through the correction — if that flips, it changes the signal. Margin compression in a quarter where input costs spike. Any corporate governance event — auditor qualifications, related-party transactions outside normal course of business.

05
Where I Stand Today
I hold this position. My average entry was ₹159.43 — approximately at the IPO price, which I read as a structural support level given the promoter buying in the same range.

My current read is that the primary base needs more time to resolve. I am not adding aggressively at this stage. If the chart develops into a confirmed Stage 2 setup — price clearing both moving averages on volume — that would change my sizing calculus. If the order book starts declining, or if JJM disbursements slow materially, I would reduce.

This is not a recommendation to anyone. My circumstances, risk tolerance, and time horizon are specific to me.
