02
Valuation as I See It

At scoring CMP ₹511, the TTM P/E was approximately 50x. That sounds expensive in isolation. But the forward P/E drops to ~30x on consensus earnings estimates, which is more aligned with what a high-quality auto ancillary with global customers and 53.8% gross margins should trade at.

The 53.8% gross margin is the key anchor here. Most auto ancillaries run 20–35% gross margins. SONA's 53.8% reflects the precision and IP content in its forgings — this is not a commodity manufacturer. That margin premium justifies a valuation premium.

The BEV revenue mix at 38% also matters for valuation. Investors increasingly discount auto ancillaries with ICE-heavy revenue. SONA's BEV mix insulates it from that discount and should, over time, attract a valuation premium as the EV transition accelerates.

Chart Structure

Some of the chart-structure thinking on this site is informed by the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data and personal use.

At scoring, SONACOMS was in Stage 2 with a near-perfect Trend Template (7/8). The 50-DMA at ₹504 was above the 200-DMA at ₹477, and the CMP at ₹511 was above both. This is the textbook Stage 2 structure.

The 52w range of ₹380–₹559 tells the story: the stock corrected from ₹559 to ₹380 (a 32% drawdown), then recovered to ₹511 at scoring with the moving averages in bullish alignment. This is a Stage 2 continuation setup — not a new breakout, but a healthy consolidation within an established uptrend.

The one criterion missing from a perfect Trend Template was the relative strength rank — it was high but not top-decile. This is the only technical caveat at scoring.

04
What Could Change My Mind

Governance escalation: If the CS resignation is followed by another KMP departure, an auditor qualification, or any unusual related-party disclosure, the -2 penalty becomes a more significant concern. I would reduce the C2 score further and potentially reduce the position.

BEV mix reversal: If the 38% BEV revenue share starts declining — because global OEMs are slowing EV production or shifting forging suppliers — the C1 full score is no longer warranted. This is the single biggest long-term risk to the thesis.

Technical break: A close below the 50-DMA (₹504) on volume would be the first technical warning. A close below the 200-DMA (₹477) would be more serious. Stage 2 breaking down would move me to reassess.

05
Where I Stand Today
I hold this position. My average entry was ₹500 — just below the CMP at scoring. I entered because the Stage 2 setup was near-perfect and the business quality is the best in the sector by my measures.

I am monitoring the governance flag. If it resolves without further events, I expect the -2 penalty to drop off the score in my next review. If it escalates, I will reassess sizing.

The BEV thesis is medium-term — the 38% revenue mix reflects where global OEMs are now, and I expect it to grow. I am not trying to trade this. This is a position I hold with a multi-quarter time horizon.
