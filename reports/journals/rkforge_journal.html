02
Valuation as I See It

The C3 score is maximum (30/30), which might seem counterintuitive for a loss-making company. My reasoning: RKFORGE is in a cyclical trough, not a structural decline. Forgings is a cyclical business — demand correlates with auto production volumes, which are near cyclical lows at the time of scoring.

The mid-cycle earnings case — normalised PAT at prior cycle peaks — implies the company trades at a very deep discount to normalised earnings. At ₹471 CMP against a promoter warrant price of ₹588, even the promoters think the stock is cheap. Promoter warrants are a binding financial commitment — this is not a verbal statement of confidence.

My research estimate of ₹900+ is a mid-cycle recovery estimate, not a going-concern valuation. It assumes PAT returns to a normal level as the auto cycle turns. That assumption may or may not be correct — I am not publishing a formal price target. But the upside from ₹471 to any reasonable mid-cycle earnings multiple is substantial.

The caveat: high C3 on a cyclical recovery thesis inflates the score. The score correctly captures the upside potential but should not be read as current fundamental strength. The C4 zero is the balancing signal — the framework correctly outputs "high potential, wrong time."

03
Chart Structure

Some of the chart-structure thinking on this site is informed by the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data and personal use.

At scoring, RKFORGE was in Stage 4 — the 52-week low was at ₹460, the CMP at ₹471 was near that low, and the 50-DMA was below the 200-DMA in a downward slope. The Trend Template scored 0/8.

Stage 4 means the stock is in a confirmed downtrend. The moving average structure (50-DMA below 200-DMA) tells me price has been under sustained distribution. Buying into Stage 4 on fundamental conviction alone is one of the more reliable ways to lose money in equity markets. I have done it before — which is why the rule is in my framework.

The technical reversal signal I am waiting for: 50-DMA turning upward, then crossing above the 200-DMA (a golden cross), ideally with the stock also clearing a prior resistance level on volume. That sequence typically takes one to three quarters after the earnings trough. If Q4 FY26 shows PAT turning positive, the technical setup should follow within a quarter or two.

04
What Could Change My Mind

Conditions that would make me look more seriously:

Q4 FY26 PAT turning positive — not just "less negative" but genuinely positive. That signals the earnings trough is behind us. Combined with a 50-DMA starting to slope upward, that's the two-condition trigger my framework requires.

A golden cross (50-DMA crossing above 200-DMA) would move the C4 score from 0 to at least 14, which would push the total score to 78 — firmly in the Strong Fit range. At that point, the setup becomes actionable.

Conditions that would remove this from my watchlist:

Additional loss quarters beyond Q3 FY26 — if PAT is still negative in Q3 and Q4, the recovery thesis is delayed further and I would reduce my conviction. Promoters converting warrants and then selling those shares would be a major reversal of the signal I am currently reading.

05
Where I Stand Today
I do not hold this position. Stage 4 with a net loss is the combination that keeps me out, regardless of how attractive the upside thesis looks on paper.

I have been tempted by recovery stories in Stage 4 before. They sometimes work — but the failure modes are more common than the successes, and the framework exists precisely to stop me from acting on temptation. The promoter warrant signal is the most bullish data point I have here. It's worth watching. It's not worth acting on yet.

I will revisit RKFORGE after Q4 FY26 results. If PAT has turned positive, I will look at the technical setup at that time. If not, I will wait another quarter. The discipline is to not act before both conditions are met.
