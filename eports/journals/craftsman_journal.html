02
Valuation as I See It

At scoring CMP ~₹7,000, the forward P/E range of 47–56x reflects the premium the market places on a genuine multi-segment manufacturer with a clean governance record and growth. This is not cheap in absolute terms. It is fair for the quality.

The EV-agnostic nature of the aluminium die casting business is worth noting specifically. Most auto ancillaries face a binary question: ICE or EV. Craftsman's answer to that question is "both" — aluminium is used extensively in both ICE and EV powertrains, transmissions, and structural components. This removes a category of risk that investors are actively pricing into ICE-heavy peers.

The storage equipment business (gravity flow racks, warehouse shelving) is the most interesting non-auto segment. Organised retail and e-commerce warehousing are secular growth themes. If this segment grows to 20%+ of revenue, it changes the business profile materially — and the multiple it deserves.

Chart Structure

Some of the chart-structure thinking on this site is informed by the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data and personal use.

At scoring CMP ~₹7,000, CRAFTSMAN was in Stage 2 in the broad sense — the 50-DMA was above the 200-DMA and price had been in an uptrend. However, the stock was correcting from its ATH of ₹8,220. That correction puts it in a base-forming process, not a breakout setup.

C4 scored 10/20 — this is the "Stage 2 correcting from ATH" category in my framework. The setup is real, the stage is right, but the base has not yet formed. In this condition, I read the correct action as watching and waiting for the base to complete, not committing capital before the setup resolves.

A breakout above ₹7,500 on 3x+ average volume would signal that the base has completed and the next leg of the Stage 2 uptrend is beginning. Below that, I am watching, not acting.

04
What Could Change My Mind

Positively: The base completes — price consolidates tightly at ₹6,800–7,200 and then breaks above ₹7,500 on significant volume. That would move C4 from 10 to 17–20, pushing the total score to 77–82. At that point, the setup is actionable.

Negatively: Price resumes the downtrend from the ATH rather than consolidating. A close below ₹6,400 on volume would tell me the base has failed and the setup I was watching for will not materialise in this cycle.

Fundamental watch: D/E creeping above 0.75x in a down cycle would concern me. The 0.58x is manageable today — if the company takes on additional debt while revenue is soft, the balance sheet risk changes character.

05
Where I Stand Today
I do not hold this position. My framework says the setup is not yet ready — the base is forming but incomplete. I am on a watchlist.

The business quality is genuine — multi-segment, EV-agnostic, strong management. The score of 70 reflects the real but incomplete setup. If the base completes and the volume confirms a new leg, I would look at this again. Until then, I am watching the chart.
