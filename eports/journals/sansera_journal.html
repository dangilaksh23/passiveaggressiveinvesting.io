Sansera manufactures precision engineered components for automotive OEMs — 2-wheelers, passenger vehicles, commercial vehicles. Customers include Husqvarna, Bajaj Auto, KTM, Honda Motorcycle, Cummins, and others. The Nichidai Corporation (Japan) joint venture is a key strategic pivot: aluminium and steel precision forgings for EV driveline components. This JV is not yet in the run-rate revenue numbers, which means it's forward optionality that the current valuation doesn't yet price.

02
Valuation as I See It

At scoring CMP ~₹2,130, the forward P/E was approximately 26x. For a company growing revenue at 24.7% YoY, that struck me as reasonably cheap — the PEG ratio is below 1.1 on that basis. This is not a deep discount setup, but it's not stretched either.

The MF holding at 26.89% tells me that institutional investors running larger mandates have independently arrived at a similar conclusion. ICRA AA is another independent validation — a credit rating agency has assessed the balance sheet as investment grade at the second highest category.

The Nichidai JV is forward optionality. It represents precision forging capability for EV driveline components — aluminium and steel. This JV has not yet hit the revenue line meaningfully at scoring time, which means the 26x P/E is a multiple on current earnings that don't include the JV contribution. If the JV ramps, the forward P/E drops.

I do not publish specific price targets in these notes. My research estimate implied a meaningful upside from the scoring CMP, primarily driven by the JV optionality and continued organic growth.

Chart Structure

Some of the chart-structure thinking on this site is informed by the stage analysis approach described in Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard by Mark Minervini (McGraw-Hill, 2013), adapted for Indian market data and personal use.

At scoring, SANSERA was in Stage 2 with the 50-DMA (₹2,029) well above the 200-DMA (₹1,615). This is the clearest bullish configuration in the Trend Template. The stock was forming VCP Base 2 near its ATH of ₹2,396 — a second base formation after the initial Stage 2 breakout.

The Trend Template scored 6/8 at scoring — two criteria below the maximum. This is constructive without being a perfect setup. The CMP range of ₹2,130–2,560 at scoring time was still within the base, not a breakout.

The 52w low of ₹1,000 shows the depth of the prior correction. The recovery from ₹1,000 to ₹2,130+ with the 50-DMA clearing the 200-DMA is the Stage 1 to Stage 2 transition the framework looks for. The base at ₹2,130–2,396 is consolidation within Stage 2 — constructive.

04
What Could Change My Mind

Hard invalidators:

Q4 FY26 PAT materially below ₹60 Cr — this would suggest the revenue growth is not translating to earnings, which breaks the valuation thesis. A delay or cancellation of the Nichidai JV — the JV is a meaningful part of why I rate this above the sector average. Either outcome would reduce my score.

Technical invalidators:

The 50-DMA breaching below the CMP on expanding volume. In a Stage 2 setup, the 50-DMA acts as the first line of support. A clean break through it with expanding volume would tell me the base has failed, which would move me to reduce.

Soft watch points:

ICE vehicle demand slowing faster than the JV timeline allows for EV revenue to replace it. Any change in promoter holding pattern — they've been stable but I watch.

05
Where I Stand Today
I hold this position. My average entry was ₹2,141.75 — I entered while the VCP base was forming, before the Trend Template reached 7/8. In retrospect, I could have waited for a higher confirmation score, but the fundamentals were strong enough that I judged the risk of missing the move to be higher than the risk of an early entry.

My current read is that the Stage 2 setup remains intact. I am watching the Q4 FY26 result and any news on the Nichidai JV ramp timeline. If the base breaks on volume, I will reassess. If Q4 delivers, I expect the setup to strengthen.
