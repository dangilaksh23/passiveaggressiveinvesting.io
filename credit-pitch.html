01 · The Problem

Your book is only as good as the file your junior picked up this morning.

A senior underwriter spends 5–6 hours on a file. A junior spends the same time but skips the customer concentration check, or pulls CIBIL but misses the supplier validation. The depth of every file depends on who happened to pick it up.

Sales cannot pre-score a lead. Ops chooses which file to process first based on intuition. The lead with the highest approval probability sits behind one that will reject in week three. That lead leaks — or it sits for days while a borrower calls a competitor.

After sanction, the book gets reviewed annually, if at all. If a borrower's top customer goes under in month five, you find out in month twelve. The DPD appears. The committee asks what was missed. The answer is usually: nothing was missed — it wasn't checked.

The real problem
This is not a hiring problem. A senior underwriter and a junior underwriter, given the same file, produce different outputs — not because one is worse at their job, but because manual underwriting at scale is structurally inconsistent. Your book pays for that inconsistency every year in write-offs you could have predicted.

Every lead in the queue has a score before it hits underwriting. Ops processes in probability order, not arrival order. Files that will reject move to a separate tray. No change to the existing underwriting process. Sales conversion improves because responses are faster. Ops throughput improves because the queue is sorted. This is Phase 1 — the fastest visible win.

09 Stage 3 — Monitoring

Same modules. Rerun on the live book quarterly. Almost free incrementally.

What IS today: The book is reviewed annually. A borrower's top customer went inactive in month five. You find out in month twelve when DPD shows up. The committee asks what was missed. The answer is: it wasn't checked — because annual reviews are all the bandwidth allows.
CIBIL shift
Director score drops 40+ points since sanction. Flagged before the next renewal cycle — not at the annual review.
Customer concentration drift
Top buyer's GST filing drops or goes inactive. The borrower's revenue base is now riskier than it was at sanction.
New litigation
Fresh case filed against the promoter or entity since the underwriting date. Surfaced before the annual review.
GST filing pattern change
Return frequency drops or turnover trend reverses. Leading indicator 3–6 months ahead of DPD.
Related-party flag
New director or entity connected to an existing NPA borrower on your book.
Supplier network degradation
Counterparties verified at sanction can no longer be confirmed. Business relationship quality is shifting.
The economics of this
These functions already exist for underwriting. Running them on the book quarterly is incremental compute, not new development. The marginal cost of monitoring 200 live accounts is a fraction of what it cost to build the underwriting engine — because you already built it.
